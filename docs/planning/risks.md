# Risk Register

This register captures the main technical and product-delivery risks known at the setup stage. Likelihood and impact are deliberately lightweight and should be revisited after feasibility work.

| Risk | Description | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Raw Input differentiation may be weaker on other hardware than on the current test setup | The current prototype supports runtime separation of multiple keyboards and mice on the tested setup, but broader hardware, driver, and connection-method coverage still needs validation. | Low | High | Keep Raw Input as the leading runtime approach, but continue testing across additional device types and connection methods before treating the result as universal. |
| Programmatic display switching may be unreliable | Prototype work showed that display inspection is useful on the tested setup, but the current simple `SetDisplayConfig` database-topology flag approach returned `ERROR_INVALID_PARAMETER (87)` for some useful switching actions. Behaviour may also vary by GPU driver, Windows version, monitor state, or HDMI/TV wake timing. | High | High | Keep the feasibility harness focused on evidence gathering, avoid assuming generic topology flags are sufficient for MVP, and validate whether a more explicit path/mode-based restore path or another OS-supported route is needed. |
| Device identifiers may not remain stable enough for persistence | Runtime identification appears feasible, but persisted identity remains sensitive to reconnects, reboots, Bluetooth re-pairing, receiver changes, or USB-port changes. | High | High | Separate runtime routing from persisted identity, prefer composite metadata over raw handles alone, and design for reconciliation or re-enrolment when identifiers change. |
| Non-keyboard or controller-style devices may not fit the main input model | Some devices may surface partially, generically, or not at all through the current keyboard/mouse-oriented Raw Input harness. | Medium | Medium | Keep the main scope focused on keyboards and pointing devices unless later research justifies broader device support. |
| HDMI or disconnected display edge cases may break switching | The desired display may be sleeping, disconnected, unavailable, or reported differently by Windows at runtime. | Medium | High | Capture edge-case evidence during feasibility work and design recovery behaviour before declaring the MVP complete. |
| Lock screen support may be infeasible | Secure desktop boundaries may prevent the application from observing input or switching displays when the workstation is locked. | Medium | Medium | Treat lock screen support as a research item rather than an MVP requirement. |
| False switching or thrashing may harm usability | Incidental activity from another zone could trigger an unwanted change or repeated switching. | Medium | High | Plan for cooldowns, priorities, explicit state tracking, and evaluation of false-switch rate as core quality measures. |
| Feasibility findings may force scope changes | Early technical evidence may invalidate some assumptions about architecture or desired features. | Medium | Medium | Keep the design modular, mark provisional decisions clearly, and allow roadmap updates based on prototype evidence. |
